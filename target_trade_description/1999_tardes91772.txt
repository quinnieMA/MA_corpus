Union Carbide Corporation is a chemicals and polymers company. The Company's principal products include Electrical and Telecommunications, Ethylene Oxide/Ethylene Glycol, Hydrocarbons, Industrial Chemicals and Polymers, Polyethylene, Solvents and Intermediates, Technology Licensing and Catalysts, Vinyl Acetate Monomer and Water Soluble Polymers. Its products are used as impact modifiers in other polymers and to produce hose and tubing, frozen-food bags and stretch wrap, as well as in applications, such as housewares, grocery sacks, trash bags, packaging, and water and gas pipes. The Company both produces and purchases ethylene from components of crude oil and natural gas. It converts ethylene to polyethylene or react it with oxygen to produce ethylene oxide, the precursor to many of the products the Company sells, such as ethylene glycol and hundreds of solvents, alcohols, surfactants, amines and specialty products. It is a wholly owned subsidiary of The Dow Chemical Company (Dow).
