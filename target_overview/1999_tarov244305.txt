This company is engaged in the manufacture and supply of aluminum and related products. It was incorporated in 1928. The US-based company has a registered office located in Richmond, Virginia. It operates in four business units: base materials; packaging and consumer; construction and distribution; and transportation. The company's base materials business unit produces metallurgical alumina, alumina chemicals, and primary aluminum. It also produces carbon products, principally for use in primary aluminum reduction plants. Reynolds' primary aluminum products include unalloyed aluminum ingot; billet, which is used by extrusion plants; sheet ingot, which is supplied to rolling facilities; foundry ingot, which is the base material for cast products, such as automotive wheels; and electrical redraw rod, which is used by the electrical cable industry. The company owns and operates carbon products manufacturing facilities in Lake Charles and Baton Rouge, Louisiana. It also sells calcined petroleum coke to the aluminum and titanium dioxide industries.
