The company, with registered business address in Seraing, Belgium, is engaged in the manufacturing of steel. The company was founded by John Cockerill, an English entrepreneur. It was established and incorporated in 1955. The company produces and distributes integrated steel and thin flat steel products. Its operations are divided into areas such as distribution of steel; aluminium and plastic products (consisting of finishing centers, warehouses and marketing agencies); covering specific geographic markets, such as Di Steel in Belgium, PUM in France and Dikema & Chabot in the Netherlands and Scandinavia; integrated steel (hot-rolling, cold-rolling and coil-coating of steel, and tinning, galvanizing and organic coating operations); automotive components (metal and plastic components such as door frames, bumpers, radiator grilles, hub caps and locks); construction (production and distribution of a variety of products for industrial, commercial and residential construction, including insulated sections, cladding, flooring and doors); mechanical engineering (boilers, shunting and diesel engines & mechanical assemblies); and services (environmental management, data processing, transportation and other). The company is considered as the largest company in the world of steel as being part of Arcelor SA. The company operates facilities in Belgium that produce flat carbon steel products, including hot and cold laminates as well as galvanized and electroplated auto-body parts.
