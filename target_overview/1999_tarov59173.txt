This company is engaged in the research, development and manufacturing of a broad range of pharmaceutical and healthcare products which are marketed throughout the world. With headquarters located in Peapack, New Jersey, United States, was founded in 1995 from the merger of Sweden's Pharmacia AB and US-based Upjohn Inc. The company is a worldwide leader in the pharmaceutical industry and ranks among the top ten pharmaceutical companies. Its products are used in the treatment of leukemia, many other types of cancer, AIDS/HIV, rheumatoid arthritis, depression, multiple sclerosis, Parkinson's disease, asthma, migraine and many other common diseases. Its products include Detrol (for treating overactive bladder), Depo-Provera (contraceptives), Rescriptor (a reverse transcriptase inhibitor used to treat aids), Genotropin, Edronax, Xalatan, Healon, Camptosar, Fragmin, Caverject, Mirapex, Estring, Dostinex, Xanax (a treatment for panic disorder), and Nicotrol. The company's nonprescription products include the baldness therapy Rogaine; Nicorette and Nicotrol smoking-cessation products; and Dramamine for motion sickness. In 1999 it also launched Celebrex for treating arthritis and osteoarthritis pain. Its products are distributed globally including Europe, Japan and the United States. This company, with manufacturing facilities in the United States, Sweden and Italy, is a wholly owned subsidiary of Pfizer Inc, the world's largest research-based biomedical and pharmaceutical company with corporate headquarters located in New York, United States.
