This company is a holding firm whose subsidiaries are engaged in the publishing and distribution of books. It is one of the United Kingdom's largest book publishers. The company was founded in 1993 through the merger of Hodder & Stoughton and Headline. It was formerly known as Hodder Headline PLC. The company has a registered office located in London, United Kingdom. The company operates its business in five divisions which include Headline, H & S General, Hachette Children's, Hodder Education, H & S Religious, Bookpoint, and Hodder Headline Ireland. Headline involves in publishing both fiction and non-fiction. It is one of the most successful, dynamic and fastest growing consumer book publishers in Britain. Hodder & Stoughton General division publishes many household names in fiction and non-fiction, which specializes in the publishing and promoting of new writers, and in creating massive bestsellers. It has been known for more than 125 years as one of the most successful imprints in British publishing. Hachette Children's Books is the star of children's publishing, which include four imprints such as Franklin Watts, Hodder Children's, Orchard Books and Wayland. Hodder Education is an education division of the company which provides high quality materials to meet the latest curriculum, academic, professional and self improvement needs in the United Kingdom and overseas. H & S Religious division publishes Christian books and Bibles. The book side is divided into two imprints, which include Hodder & Stoughton, for books aimed at a general readership, such as biographies, anthologies, gift books, parenting, relationships, and self-help; and Hodder Christian Books, which include books of a more overtly religious focus, often from the evangelical tradition. Bookpoint supplies the distribution services. Hodder Headline Ireland division was established to publish the best of Irish non-fiction and fiction, popular and literary.
